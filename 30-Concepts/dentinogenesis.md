# Dentinogenesis (odontoblast differentiation)

> The formation of dentine — the mineralised tissue beneath enamel — driven by the differentiation of neural-crest-derived dental mesenchymal cells into matrix-secreting, mineralising odontoblasts.

## Definition

Dentinogenesis begins when neural-crest-derived mesenchymal cells in the dental papilla and pulp, signalled by inner enamel epithelium, differentiate into pre-odontoblasts; after polarisation these mature and secrete extracellular matrix, initiating mineralisation that transforms predentin into mature dentine ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). The process is governed by a temporally and spatially ordered network of pathways including WNT, BMP/TGF-β, MAPK, NOTCH and NF-κB ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## Why it matters

Disrupted signalling during dentine formation causes **dentine dysplasia** — aberrant mineralisation, diminutive roots, rapid tooth attrition — a common manifestation of congenital syndromes ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). Understanding the regulators (beyond known mutational genes like DSPP, COL1A1/2, DMP1) opens therapeutic and regenerative targets ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## Lineage and progenitors

Single-cell analysis of human odontogenic tissue places **EFNB2+ mesenchymal cells** at the root of the odontoblast trajectory as the primary multipotent progenitor (highest stemness, CellRank starting state), bifurcating toward pre-odontoblasts/peri-odontoblastic layers versus stromal remodelling ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). Odontoblast markers used to track differentiation include ALP, DSPP, DMP1 and BGLAP ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## Regulation by STAT3

[[stat3]] is required for odontoblast commitment; its loss (in silico, in vitro, or via `Stat3fl/fl;OsxCre` mice) impairs differentiation and causes dentine dysplasia (reduced dentine width, short roots, delayed eruption), acting through [[wnt-beta-catenin-signalling]] via WNT2B ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). The mouse phenotype recapitulates clinical dentine dysplasia type I ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

# Wnt/β-catenin signalling

> The canonical Wnt pathway — Wnt ligands stabilising β-catenin to drive TCF/LEF-mediated transcription — which in dentine development Zhu et al. show is activated downstream of STAT3 via the ligand WNT2B.

## Definition

Canonical Wnt signalling is driven by Wnt ligands acting through receptors to stabilise β-catenin (CTNNB1), which activates β-catenin/TCF/LEF1-mediated transcription of target genes ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). In odontogenesis, this pathway drives transcription of odontogenic effectors such as DSPP and DMP1, and is essential for odontoblast differentiation ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## Why it matters

It is the downstream effector through which [[stat3]] controls [[dentinogenesis]] ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). STAT3 knockdown synchronously downregulated canonical components (CTNNB1, AXIN2, LEF1) and upregulated the Wnt inhibitor SOST, while overexpressing the ligand WNT2B partially rescued the STAT3-loss phenotype — placing Wnt/β-catenin squarely on the STAT3→dentine causal chain ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## WNT2B as the STAT3 target

WNT2B (a.k.a. WNT13) is a Wnt ligand previously linked to mineralisation and mesenchymal stem-cell differentiation but with no prior reported role in tooth development ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). Zhu et al. identify it as the most likely direct STAT3 target in dental pulp cells: it is the top Wnt-pathway DEG on STAT3 knockdown, STAT3 binds its promoter, and its overexpression rescues differentiation defects ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]). Other Wnt components also fell on knockdown (WNT16, GPC4, RAC2, PRICKLE1, SERPINF1) ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

## Contested points

The WNT2B rescue is only **partial**, leaving open whether WNT2B alone mediates the phenotype or acts alongside the other downregulated Wnt components; an in vivo Wnt2b overexpression model is proposed but not yet done ([[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]]).

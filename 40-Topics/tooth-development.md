# Tooth development

> The biology of how teeth form — here focused on dentinogenesis, the differentiation of dental mesenchymal progenitors into mineralising odontoblasts, and the signalling that regulates it.

## Core concepts

- [[dentinogenesis]] — dentine formation and odontoblast differentiation; the central process
- [[stat3]] — transcription factor driving odontoblast lineage commitment
- [[wnt-beta-catenin-signalling]] — the effector pathway (via WNT2B) downstream of STAT3

## Sources, by sub-theme

### Regulators of odontoblast differentiation

- [[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]] — establishes STAT3 as a key regulator of dentinogenesis, acting through WNT2B/canonical Wnt; conditional-knockout mice phenocopy dentine dysplasia type I.

### Single-cell landscapes of dental tissue

- [[10-Summaries/zhu-2026-stat3-dentinogenesis]] — builds a single-cell transcriptional landscape of human odontogenic tissue (from GEO GSE146123), identifying EFNB2+ mesenchymal cells as the primary odontoblast progenitor.
